4D blogging

Copy is just the beginning

TL;DR Four Dimensional blogging says posts should be copy and code. Copy is just the beginning. The next step is to enhance your copy with custom code because that's what we We who love to code should add it to what We know code how to code and should use it creatively too.

Many Web leaders will say content is king. Start with the content. Then, take that content and consider how to present it semantically in HTML. Only after the content is semantic should the developer consider enforcing the content's with CSS. JavaScript is considered an enhancement and should be used last.

Most of the developers I know don't actively blog. They say it's because they don't know what to write. What they're referring to is the copy. They know how to write the remaining stuff.

What if developers thought of blogging as a way to write all four dimensions (4D). Afterall, each of those four dimensions is a literacy in of itself. Why pretend they're not??

Many developers who do blog say to 'write for yourself' because they say nobody is actually going to read it anyway. Well, take that to the extreme and write things that are invisible to the reader. Focus on writing the underlying code to support the content!

This might sound like a lot of work. It's true that it's more work than writing a tweet and forgetting about it. 4D blogging means you are practicing all your writing in small segments and letting each piece support the previous.

Rant because I can!

Progressive Enhancement says that your content does not need to look the same on every device, browser, or connection speed. Take that philosophy and put it to your blog posts. You don't need a perfect template. This is your blog, dammit! Your creative tools are text, code, and your imagination. Blog templates are for people who don't know how to do what you do. They are illiterate to HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. That is why platforms like Medium exist. For people who can't code. But you can code. And you should. You don't write the exact same lines of copy for each blog and you shouldn't write the exact same lines of code either. Blogging is a creative process and you shouldn't worry about effeciecy, templates, or anything else. This is your creative space. Let your mind wander into words to say, or funky animations. OR some weird transition you think would be cool to drive home an image. You get. it. Let each blog post you create be something new.

Logistics

So, I've started doing this using blogger. But their HTML and workflow is terrible. I tend to code in Atom then export etc. But that is painful. Then, I thought about Codepen. I just discovered that CodePen will export this content into a Gist. That's handy. As this post, which I'm currenly writing on CodePen is demonstrating (to myself). But Codepen is not really in blogform to be shareable with the world. (Maybe it is but - I mean moreso in the tranditional blog format.) For example, Grandma would get lost here so I'd rather send her a traditional URL to a single page she can click around. That is's a great place to write drafts. My thought is I will export to gist then copy.

But here's my other point. That rant above about logistics was just mental vomit. Where else am I going to type it than here and now. But that just exhasusted my writing muscles. I want to go back to coding. So, I just wrapped this whole thing in a '.draft' class and going to edit it with something cool. Why? Because this is my blog and I'm allowed to post rants and incomplete thoughts. feel free to vomit copy all over your blog and come back to edit it later.

Learning to love

The purpose of education is to improve the quality of living. Schools exist because people are naturally ignorant. The same holds true with people's understanding of character and love. How then can a person learn to love? Love is more than a feeling. Feelings are not always aligned with love. If the second highest commandment

“Teacher, which is the great commandment in the Law?” And he said to him, “You shall love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your mind. This is the great and first commandment. And a second is like it: You shall love your neighbor as yourself. On these two commandments depend all the Law and the Prophets.”

Matthew 22:36-40

 is to love our neighbor as ourselves then it would make sense that we know when we are in fact following that command.

So, how do we learn to love?

First off, love is between people. As such, learning to love means understanding people. Second, love is complicated and requires more than pithy sayings with photos behind them. Love is a subject that must be studied if it is to impact another person. So, where do I suggest we find such a guide? If you know me then you probably already know I'm going to say "The bible". But I'm not going to be that vague. Specifically, I believe we can learn to love from reading Genesis. The book is filled with sensational stories between nearly every relational situation. The attentive reader will be filled with intense thoughts and emotions while reading of life, murder, envy, failure, blessing, curse, love, hate, war, betrayal, anger, and more. (That summary reminds me of the opening of the Princess Bride.) As such, The text gives us a chance to be honest with ourselves by awakening our soul. Those thoughts and emotions are for more than entertainment. To those who would sincerely study love might consider extracting the many morals contained in each of the stories. There are underlying relational laws that are broken, upheld, and redeemed. All which teach us what to do and not do if we are to love our neighbor as much as we love ourself.

6 months to 40

I am six months away from turning 40. I’ve been looking forward to 40 for 20 years. My dad taught me a Time Frame that summarized life in decades. He said that my 20’s were preparatory years and my 30’s were initiating years. Those two decades got me ready to spend my 40’s and 50’s in the phase known as domination. I couldn't wait for domination! One of the side effects of the Time Frame was that it helped me keep the majority of thoughts to myself because I believed many of my views were still in progress. I still believe that life builds on experience but I’ve since abandoned the expectation of domination. (But that's for another post..)
One of the details of the bible that people tend to doubt is the ages of mankind at the beginning of time. Adam and friends lived well into 900 years old. People used to wait until they turned 100 to have children. Accepting their ages makes me think that just because the body dies much younger doesn’t mean we attain wisdom much quicker. So, even turning 40, I have an believe that in the grand scheme of things I’m just a toddler in time. My only desire turning 40 is that I can lead my sons in the ways of YHVH. Here’s to Phase III.
