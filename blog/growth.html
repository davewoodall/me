Growth

When something that used to be difficult or time consuming becomes easy to accomplish.

HTML is the New English

Consider how difficult it is for people in America who don't know English. That is how I felt about the Internet before I learned to code. I knew how to consume the web, but trying to convey what I wanted to was impossible.

I wanted to live there without being bound to a translator. I describe the web as a country called 'InternetLand'. In InternetLand, HTML is the new English and I sensed there was more opportunity in InternetLand than in America. So I sold my business and set sail for a new life as a developer. That makes my career path from graphic designer through entrepreneurship to programmer.

Now that I know the languages means I can focus on the ideas. I am now able to be creative again. This ability to freely speak online without use of a social network is exteremly liberating. Every account I delete is an investment back into concentration which is a hot commodity these days.

Practice makes programmers

Which leads me here. The more I code, the better I get. I've already started fake farm as a way to practice. But that is a different neighborhood of coding. This freshly pressed magazine you're reading is another way for me to practice. This is frontend - html, css, javascript, plus content. I call it 4 dimensional blogging.
